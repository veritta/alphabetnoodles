Nuclear Decay

2018
I pour the memory of your laugh into a bottle and knock it back like vodka. I hate vodka, but it does the trick, and in much the same fashion I find my throat burning for a few searing moments, before my head fills up with cotton wool and I feel just disconnected enough from it all. I wipe your smile from my lips and wash it down the drain, spitting out the last of your kisses as I watch the colours ooze across the ceramic of the sink, white like the whites of your wide eyes, white like the void you painted onto my skin. The feeling of time itches like a poorly-made coat and I shrug it off, shedding your touch and the shape of your name, leaving it spooled like snakeskin on the floor where you last lay in my arms.

You can ride it if you like

2018

It starts like a caffeine buzz, like anxiety gnawing away at your stomach, like something crawling over your skin, and it’s just like limerence, you feel so sick but you also feel good, heady with anticipation, vision shimmering, every nerve alight with potential.

It pulls you in like eye contact with your crush, and with so much and so little warning — you knew this was coming, but it’s always a ride — it consumes you, painting your insides anew.

The world is brighter and the edges not so defined, and everything is moving, everything is alive, and now it feels like the entirety of human existence is on your side, that the whole cosmically hilarious black humour of life has been worth it, just for these moments — minutes — hours — or was it seconds?

And yes, you’ll wake the next day with naught but memories, but that’s all life is in the end, nothing but memories — nothing but memories, and the ability to see something that you didn’t before.
